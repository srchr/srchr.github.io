Our Biers



The Bavarians introduced the Reinheitsgebot (Purity Laws) in 1487.
These laws stipulated that only water, barley, and hops were allowed in the making of Bier.
Today, half a millennium later, the laws still apply to the brewing of the Biers we serve at the Schnitzelhaus.
Further to that, we've thrown off the restrictive shackles of Munich's corporate breweries and spread our wings to
source the very finest Biers, schnapps and liquors from the best boutique producers in Bavaria.

We're independent. We're free. We're happy. And it shows.

Try our authentic Bavarian food recipes

Schnitzelhaus has decided to share some of its beloved Bavarian food recipes so you can try Bavaria's wonderful cuisine at home!

Rieser Kachle


"Rieser Kiachle" are a local speciality of Noerdlingen on the Romantic Road and are favoured with locals and visitors alike.
This doughnut like cake that is also known as "Auszogne", "Pfannkuchen" or "Krapfen" is traditional to Bavaria and most particularly to the Rieser area.

    Ingredients (for approx. 12)

  • 500g flour
  • 250ml milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 50g butter
  • 40g sugar
  • 20g yeast
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • approx. 1kg deep fry fat

    Preparation

  • Mix the ingredients to a light dough and kneed well
  • Divide into 12 equally sized portions and roll each into a ball
  • Leave to rise at room temperature
  • With greased fingers pull each of the dough balls out to a circle of some 15 cm creating a thin bit in the centre that is almost transparent
  • Fry in a deep fat fryer (180 C) ensuring that the edges go golden brown whilst the centre stays white
  • Drain on kitchen paper and leave to cool
  • Dust with sugar or icing sugar before serving


Bavarian Leberkaese


Leberkaese is a meatloaf with a really fine texture. You could compare it to maybe the texture of bologna or a frankfurter only the flavor is much different.
This is popular in many parts of Germany as well as in Austria mostly as a breakfast and lunch meal. It is cut hot right out of the oven and enjoyed with a medium or spicy mustard.
For breakfast it is thinly sliced and served with a platter of smoked sausage. In Bavaria a thumbs width slice is served on a roll and eaten much like we would a hamburger.

    Ingredients

  • 400g lean beef
  • 400g pork
  • 200g bacon (with rind removed)
  • 250ml iced water
  • 1tsp salt
  • 1tsp freshly ground pepper
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1dstspn marjoram
  • Butter to grease the tin

    Preparation

  • All meat must be well cooled before preparation begins
  • Cut meat into chunks and mince on a fine setting
  • Mix together both meats and add salt, pepper and the iced water until smooth
  • Mince the bacon on a medium setting and mix with the meat
  • Peel and grate the onion and add to the mixture together with the marjoram
  • Place in the fridge to cool
  • Put the mixture into a greased loaf tin
  • Smooth the surface and, using the blade of a knife, make a diamond-shaped pattern across the top (about 5 mm deep)
  • Bake in the centre of a preheated oven (180 C) for about an hour (fan oven: approx. 160 C, gas mark 2 to 2.5)


Crispy Schupfnudeln


Leberkaese is a meatloaf with a really fine texture. You could compare it to maybe the texture of bologna or a frankfurter only the flavor is much different.
This is popular in many parts of Germany as well as in Austria mostly as a breakfast and lunch meal. It is cut hot right out of the oven and enjoyed with a medium or spicy mustard.
For breakfast it is thinly sliced and served with a platter of smoked sausage. In Bavaria a thumbs width slice is served on a roll and eaten much like we would a hamburger.

    Ingredients

  • 400 g floury potatoes
  • 50 g soft butter
  • 2 medium sized egg yolks
  • 150 g flour
  • 3 dstspn flour
  • 40 g melted butter
  • Salt, pepper, nutmeg

    Preparation

  • Boil the potatoes in their skins, peel, mash and leave to cool
  • Add the soft butter, egg yolks and flour
  • Season with salt, pepper and nutmeg
  • Knead until smooth and form into balls, cover and leave to stand for 15 minutes
  • On a floured surface cut the dough into pieces and roll into bullet shaped noodles (Schupfnudeln)
  • Mix 250 ml of cold water with 3 dstspns flour and add to a pan of 3 l of boiling salted water.
  • Boil the noodles in the water in 3 portions until thy rise to the surface
  • Remove from the water and drain
  • Toss in melted butter
  • Serve with venison stew or roasted or as part of a dessert with stewed fruit


Schneeflocken


These biscuits are ideal to make together with children. They just melt in your mouth as if they really were made out of snow! And they taste just out of this world!

    Ingredients

  • 250g butter
  • 100g icing sugar
  • A few drops of vailla essence
  • 100g plain flour
  • 250g cornflour
  • 50g ground almonds

    Preparation

  • Mix all ingredients well and leave to cool for 30 minutes in a cool place
  • Shape walnut-sized balls from the dough
  • Place on a baking tray lined with baking paper ensuring that there is plenty of room between the biscuits
  • Imprint stripes onto the balls with a floured fork if you wish (they taste just as good with or without the stripes!)
  • Bake at 175 C for approximatelt 20 minutes
